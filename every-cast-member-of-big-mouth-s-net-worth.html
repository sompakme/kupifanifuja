Big Mouth is a Netflix Original that highlights all of the worst--and best--parts of being an awkward teenager during the transition from kid to adult. There are first-time boyfriend-girlfriend relationships, hormone monsters, and lots of hilarious (and sometimes weird) situations. The show follows Nick, Andrew, Jay, and Jesse, all young teens, and friends that go through all the drama that is high school. They go through scenarios like getting your first period and exploring your body for the first time. The show has become relatively popular since its release in 2017; the cast has also been successful. Here are all the net worths of the cast from Big Mouth.

10. Jason Mantzoukas - $500,000

If you've ever seen a curly-haired, eccentric and over the top character, you may have seen Jason Mantzoukas. Known for his roles in Brooklyn Nine-Nine, The Good Place, and Modern Family, Jason always plays the funniest, and sometimes the oddest, characters. In Big Mouth, Jason plays a character named Jay, who is the 'gross' one in the group. The episodes that revolve around Jay always seem to include his crazy brothers and neglectful mother, or his dog with self-esteem issues. In short, Jay's life doesn't seem like it's going all that great.

9. David Thewlis - $800,000

David Thewlis is a British actor, director, screenwriter, and author. His most notable role was the long-running character of Remus Lupin from the Harry Potter films, but he can also be seen in The Theory of Everything and The Boy in the Striped Pyjamas. In Big Mouth, though, David Thewlis plays the Shame Wizard, a phantom that is from the same universe as Maury the Hormone Monster. His job is to make all of the kids feel shame for all the things they have done wrong. As infuriating the character can be, you learn to love him in the end.

8. John Mulaney - $3 million

Andrew Glouberman, one of the main characters in Big Mouth, is Nick's best friend and is the first person in the main friend group to hit puberty. His hormone monster, Maury, follows him around, showing up only when he gets 'aroused'.

7. Jenny Slate - $5 million

Born as Jenny Sarah Slate in 1982, this talented actress, comedian, and author has been in the business since 2005 and was even featured on Saturday Night Live as several characters from 2009 to 2010. She has a lot of experience in voice acting since some of her most important roles were in animated shows like Bob's Burgers, Zootopia, and The Secret Life of Pets and much more, including her role as Missy Foreman-Greenwald in Big Mouth. Missy is an awkward young girl and is known for being a bit of a nerd, but the character is loveable, overall.

Jessi Klein's filmography is not extremely extensive, especially compared to some of the other people on this list, but she definitely is an influential comedy writer, voice actress, and stand-up comic. She was also a writer for Saturday Night Live from 2009 to 2010. As for her slightly smaller acting profile, she has been featured in productions like Lucy, the Daughter of the Devil, Premium Blend, and Michael and Michael Have Issues.
