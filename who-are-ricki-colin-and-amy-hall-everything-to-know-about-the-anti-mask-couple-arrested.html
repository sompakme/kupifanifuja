Ricki Colin and Amy Hall are the anti-mask couples arrested after attacking the owner of a cookie shop for enforcing the mask law. The following is information on the arrest case that you may be interested in.

On Wednesday, November 3, a pair of anti-maskers were arrested after they recorded themselves attacking a cookie shop owner in Eugene, Oregon.

The duo is shown strolling inside the store in video footage that has gone popular on social media, and the owner requests them to leave since they are not wearing masks.

After the mask policy announcement, the store has been targeted by prank calls, phone bombers, stalkers, and various sorts of harassment in recent months.

The bakery got an outpouring of support from netizens, causing the firm to briefly shut down its online store to keep up with the orders.

Who Are Ricki Colin And Amy Hall?

Ricki Colin, 34, and Amy Hall, 45, have been identified as the people that entered Crumb Together without wearing a mask.

They are both from Portland and are accused of robbery and violence.

Eugene Oregan Anti Mask Couple Arrest Details

As per the police, anti-mask pair Ricki Colin and Amy Hall were both arrested and brought to the Lane Country Jail following the incident.

Likewise, Collin was charged with robbery in the third degree after walking away with the bat. The business owner had cuts and bruises during the incident and was sent to the hospital for treatment.

In the video, the store owner constantly urges the couple to leave and directs them to the door. At one point, Amy shoves the shop owner, who then pulls a baseball bat from behind the counter, but the pair refuses to move. As Colin continues to video, a violent fight breaks out between the store owner and Amy.

Later, the couple flagged down nearby police aftermath of the attack and gave them the footage of the incident to review. However, they were arrested on the spot.
