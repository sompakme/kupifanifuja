Young Heather Graham

Heather Graham was born in Milwaukee, WI, in January 1970. That makes her around 20 to 30 years old in the first group of pictures. During that time frame, Heather Graham was really busy! Here is a quick run-down of some of her work in that decade. In 1988's action film License to Drive, Heather played Mercedes Lane in her first prominent leading role. She also appeared in the satirical black comedy Heathers that same year.

Graham then appeared as Nadine in the crime drama Drugstore Cowboy, and soon after, she joined the cast of I Love You to Death with William Hurt and Keanu Reeves. She went on to play Annie Blackburn in 1992's Twin Peaks: Fire Walk with Me, and then co-starred with James Woods in the sports-comedy drama Diggstown. In 1993, Heather Graham appeared in Six Degrees of Separation, The Ballad of Little Jo, and Even Cowgirls Get the Blues.

After taking a small break, she returned in 1997 with Boogie Nights, Nowhere, and Scream 2. Two Girls and a Guy and Lost in Space were released in 1998. Then came the popular 1999 film Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me.

Heather Graham In The Early 2000s

Committed marked the beginning of Heather Graham's second significant decade. Her portrayal of an unhappy married woman in Sidewalks of New York came next. She worked on many major motion pictures in the early 2000s, such as Killing Me Softly, From Hell, Anger Management, Say It Isn't So, The Guru, and Hope Springs.

She portrayed Dr. Molly Clock in the NBC TV series Scrubs from 2004 to 2005. Graham also made an appearance in the movie Mary and an episode of Arrested Development during this time. In the American films Bobby, Gray Matters, Adrift in Manhattan, Broken, and Miss Conception, she co-starred. She portrayed Jade in the 2009 film The Hangover. She didn't appear in the film's sequel, but she returned as Jade in the third and final installment of the trilogy, The Hangover Part III.
