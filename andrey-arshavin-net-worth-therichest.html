Andrey Arshavin is a Russian former professional football player and he has an estimated net worth of $13.5 million. Born Andrey Sergeyevich Arshavin on May 29, 1981 in Leningrad (now Saint Petersburg), his father, who was an amateur football player, encouraged him to pursue a professional career in football. He began playing football at an early age and, by the age of seven, he was enrolled in the Smena football academy of Zenit in his hometown.

In 2000, Arshavin made his debut when he was included in the first-team squad and earned his first win with a 3-0 against the English side Bradford City in the Intertoto Cup. He came in the game as a first-half substitute for Andrey Kobelev.

In 2009, Andrey Arshavin, signed for Arsenal from Zenit St Petersburg in a £12m deal that runs until summer 2012. The Russian earns £70,000 a week and is sponsored by Nike during his time with the Gunners. He wore the number 23 shirt, which was last worn by Sol Campbell who left the team for Portsmouth. It was announced in 2013 that Arshavin signed a two-year deal with Zenit and moved back to the team on a free transfer.

On international level, Andrey made his debut with the Russian national team in 2002 but he was not included at the 2002 FIFA World Cup and Euro 2004. His first international goal was made during a friendly match versus Romania in February the following year. He was named the national captain during a Euro 2008 qualifier against Estonia. Guus Hiddink named him Russia's first-choice captain on June 11, 2009.
